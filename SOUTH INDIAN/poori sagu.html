Poori Sagu

a deep-fried bread made from unleavened whole-wheat flour that originated in the Indian subcontinent.


Ingredients

For Poori

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Oil, for deep-frying

For Sagu

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

For Poori

  • In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  • Gradually add the water, kneading until the dough comes together.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • Divide the dough into small balls and roll out each ball into a thin circle.
  • Heat the oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  • Fry the puris in batches until golden brown and puffed up.
  • Drain the puris on paper towels.

For sagu

  • Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds and cook until they splutter.
  • Add the onion and cook until softened.
  • Add the green chilies and ginger and cook for 1 minute.
  • Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, and asafoetida and cook for 1 minute.
  • Add the mashed potatoes and mix well.
  • Add salt to taste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with puris.

Tips:

  • For fluffier puris, knead the dough until it is smooth and elastic.
  • To prevent the puris from sticking to the pan, add a few drops of oil to the pan before frying.
  • If you don't have asafoetida, you can substitute with 1/4 teaspoon ground fenugreek seeds.
  • You can add other vegetables to the sagu, such as carrots, peas, or cauliflower.
