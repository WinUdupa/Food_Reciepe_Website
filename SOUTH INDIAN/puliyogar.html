Puliyogare

Puliyogare or Tamarind Rice is a tangy-spicy South Indian Rice variety, which is known for its long shelf life and great travel food.


Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/4 cup jaggery
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp chana dal
  • 1/2 tsp urad dal
  • 1 dry red chili
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  • Wash and soak the rice in water for 30 minutes.
  • In a small bowl, combine the tamarind pulp and jaggery. Add 1/2 cup of water and mix well. Soak for 15 minutes.
  • After 15 minutes, squeeze the tamarind pulp and extract the juice. Discard the pulp
  • Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds, chana dal, urad dal, and dry red chili. Fry until the mustard seeds pop and the chana dal turns golden brown.
  • Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala powder. Fry for a few seconds
  • Add the tamarind juice and salt to taste. Bring to a boil.
  • Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes
  • Drain the rice and add it to the tamarind mixture. Mix well.
  • Cover the pan and cook over low heat for 10 minutes, or until the rice is heated through.
  • Stir in the cilantro and serve hot.

Tips:

  • For a spicier puliyogare, add more dry red chili to the tempering.
  • If you don't have tamarind pulp, you can use 2 tablespoons of tamarind paste instead.
  • You can also add other vegetables to the puliyogare, such as carrots, peas, or potatoes.
  • Puliyogare can be served with raita, papad, and pickle.
