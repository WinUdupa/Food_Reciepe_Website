Poha

It is made from rice (Oryza sativa) and is one of the major food products of India. It is known by different names all over India


Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of thick poha(beaten ricce flakes)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 to 3 green chilles, slit or chopped
  • 1 spring curry leaves
  • 1 medium potato(optional), cubed to 1/2 inch (or 1/2 cup mixed vegetables)
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons of peanut or cashew
  • 3/4 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 3/4 teaspoon cumin seeds(jeera)
  • 1 pinch hing
  • 1/3 teaspoon salt(adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 or 1 teaspoon sugar(optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • Lemon juice as needed
  • 2 tablespoons coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions

  • Rinse the poha in a strainer under running water until it is softened. Drain well.
  • Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the peanuts or cashews and fry until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  • Add the mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and hing to the pan. Once the mustard seeds start to pop, add the curry leaves and green chilies. Fry for a few seconds.
  • Add the onions and cook until softened and translucent.
  • Add the potatoes (if using) and cook until golden brown and tender.
  • Add the turmeric powder and salt. Mix well.
  • Add the poha and mix well. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the poha is heated through.
  • Add the lemon juice, sugar (if using), and coriander leaves. Mix well and serve hot.

Tips:

  • For a spicier kanda poha, add more green chilies or red chili powder
  • You can also add other vegetables to the kanda poha, such as carrots, peas, or tomatoes.
  • Kanda poha can be served with a side of raita, chutney, and pickle.
