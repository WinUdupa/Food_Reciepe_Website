Rice and Rasam

Rasam is a spicy South Indian soup-like dish. It is usually served as a side dish with rice in a traditional South Indian meal


Ingredients

For rice

  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

For Rasam

  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon rasam powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon tamarind paste
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions

  • To make the rice, wash the rice and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.
  • Drain the rice and add it to a pot with 2 cups of water.
  • Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-12 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through.
  • To make the rasam, heat the oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add the onions and cook until they are translucent.
  • Add the tomatoes, ginger, garlic, rasam powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and tamarind paste.
  • Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the tomatoes are softened.
  • Add 2 cups of water and salt to taste.
  • Bring the rasam to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the flavors have melded together.
  • Garnish with cilantro and serve hot with rice.

Tips:

  • For a more flavorful rasam, you can roast the tomatoes and onions before adding them to the pan.
  • If you don't have rasam powder, you can make your own by dry roasting and grinding coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, black peppercorns, and red chilies.
  • You can adjust the spiciness of the rasam to your liking by adding more or less red chili powder.
  • If you want a thicker rasam, you can mash a few of the tomatoes before adding them to the pan.
  • You can also add other vegetables to the rasam, such as carrots, potatoes, or drumsticks.
