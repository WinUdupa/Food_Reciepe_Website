Bisibelebath

it is an excotic south indian cuisine special to karnataka. It is rich in its flavour and nutrients


Ingredients

  • 1 Cup of rice
  • 1/2 cup toor dal(split pigeon peas)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal(split black gram)
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 potato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peas, frozen or fresh
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatos
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1 spring curry leaves
  • salt to taste
  • tamarind extract to taste

Instructions

  • Wash the rice, toor dal, and urad dal thoroughly
  • Soak the rice and toor dal in seperate bowls for atleast 30min
  • Soak the urad dal in a seperate bowl for atleast 6 hours, or overnight
  • Drain the rice and toor dal
  • Drainthe urad dal and grinf it into fine paste
  • Heat the oil in a large pot over medium flame
  • Add the Mustard seeds and cumin seeds and cook until they splutter
  • Add the asafaoetida and curry leaves and cook for a minute
  • Add the onion and cook until softened
  • Add the tomatoes and cook until sofetened
  • Add the turmeric powder, red chilli powder, and garam masala powder and cook for a minute
  • Add the rice, toor dal, urad dal paste, carrots, potatoes, green beans and peas
  • Add enough water to cover the ingredients by 2 inches
  • Bring the mixture to boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25min, or until the rice and lentils are cooked
  • Add salt to taste and tamarind extract to your desired taste
  • serve hot

Tips:

  • You can also add other vegetables to the bisibelebath such as cauliflower, cabbage, or spinach
  • If you don't have urad dal, you can substitute with mansoor dal(split red lentil)
  • If you don't have tamarind extract, you can substitute with lemon juice or vinegar.
  • You can adjust the spiceness by adding more or less red chilli powder
