Manglore Buns

it is an excotic south indian cuisine special to karnataka. It is rich in its flavour and nutrients


Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups maida
  • 1 cup curd
  • 1/2 cup gram flour
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1 teaspoon jeer
  • 2 green chillies, finely chopped
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  • In a large bowl, combine the maida, curd, gram flour, rice flour, onion, coriander leaves, coconut, jeera seeds, green chillies, curry leaves, and salt.
  • Mix well until all the ingredients are combined.
  • Add water as needed to make a thick batter.
  • Heat the oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  • To test the oil, drop a small amount of batter into it. If the batter rises to the top and floats, the oil is ready.
  • Drop the batter by spoonfuls into the hot oil.
  • Fry the bajjis in batches until golden brown on both sides.
  • Remove the bajjis from the oil and drain them on paper towels.
  • Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Tips:

  • For a crispier bajji, use a mixture of gram flour and rice flour.
  • If you want a spicier bajji, add more green chili powder.
  • You can also add other vegetables to the bajji batter, such as potatoes, carrots, or peas.
  • Mangalore bajji can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
